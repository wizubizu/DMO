About Me

Born 27th July, 1982 in Okuku, Odo-Otin LGA of Osun State Nigeria, Dr Michael Olugbile is a Harvard-trained World Bank development expert with a passion to make a positive difference in the lives of the common man. He is a naturally born leader who is totally committed to providing people-oriented service in a transparent and accountable manner with utmost fear of God.

Apart from his professional experience of furthering the well-being of populations in Nigeria and around the world through his work with the World Bank, UK-DFID, USAID, Global Fund and Government of Nigeria (LAUTECH Teaching Hospital, Osogbo; National Hospital, Abuja; National Malaria Control Program; SURE-P etc), Dr Olugbile has an extensive leadership experience including serving as President of Ilorin University Medical Students Association (ILUMSA), Vice-President of University of Ilorin Students Union Government (SUG), President of Harvard School of Public Health (HSPH) Nigerian Students and Scholars Society, Life President of Harvard (HSPH) Class of 2011, Councillor, HSPH Alumni Council among several others.

As a naturally born dependable leader, Dr Olugbile's transparency, accountability, problem-solving, people oriented, servant-leadership attributes have distinguished him in every leadership position he has occupied.

Having lost his father at the age of seven, Olugbile and his seven siblings had a harrowing experience growing up. This experience instilled in him from a young age, a heart for the less-privileged in the society such that while in Medical School, he used part of the proceeds of the scholarships he enjoyed from Living Faith Church to establish Dr Olugbile Scholarship Foundation (DOSF). The Foundation provides educational support for fellow poor students in his community through award of scholarships and payment of UME and WAEC exams fees.

In furtherance of Dr Olugbile's passion for the development of the people at the grassroots, Dr Olugbile and his lovely wife, Mrs Oluwapelumi Olugbile (formerly Miss Oluwapelumi Olutoye) founded a non-governmental organization named Grassroot Initiative for Positive Socioeconomic Development (GIPSED) also known as Society for Positive Change. The organization with the overall aim of improving the lives of the people at the grassroots will be implementing educational, economic empowerment, political, health, sport and agricultural projects. GIPSED is duly registered with Corporate Affairs Commission.
