Services to Humanity



Dr. Michael Opeyemi Olugbile (DMO) is a Harvard-trained World Bank development expert with a passion to make a positive difference in the lives of the common man. He is a natural born leader who is totally committed to providing people-oriented service in a transparent and accountable manner with utmost fear of God. He is a scholar and leader par excellence.

He is Senior Technical Adviser to the Osun State Governor on Development Partners and International Relations

Dr Olugbile’s experience cuts across different sectors and geographical locations. With local and international experience, he has contributed immensely to the well-being of the general population. He currently works as a Health Specialist and Consultant with the World Bank in the Health, Nutrition and Population (HNP) division.

Some of his other work experiences include;

  • Research Fellow –Brigham and Women’s Hospital. Boston, MA, USA.
  • Research Collaborator – Global Fund Malaria Evaluation Project, Boston, MA, USA.
  • Assistant Project Coordinator – Michigan State University Research Project.
  • Team Lead – Global Fund Country Coordinating Mechanism (CCM), Quarterly Oversight Assessment.
  • Health Financing Consultant- Abt Associates Bethesda Maryland, USA.
  • Consultant – Futures’ Institute, Connecticut, USA.
  • Consultant – SURE-P Maternal and Child Health, Abuja.
  • Trainee Consultant – Harvard School of Public Health (HSPH ) China Initiative, Beijing, China.
  • Lead Researcher – Malaria Control Research Network – Boston, MA, USA.
  • Research Fellow - National Malaria Control Programme, Abuja.
  • Medical Practitioner – General Hospital Asubiaro, Osogbo, Osun State; LAUTECH Teaching Hospital Osogbo; National Hospital Abuja.
  • Medical Officer-in-charge, Evening Light Hospital, Abuja.
  • Project Coordinator – Medical Missions, NCCMDS-UITH.
  • Secondary School Teacher – Odo-Otin Grammar School and Oyinlola Comprehensive High School, Okuku.

Apart from his professional experience of furthering the well-being of populations in Nigeria and around the world, Dr Olugbile has an extensive leadership experience including the following;

  • Member – Harvard School of Public Health Alumni Association, Feedback and Assessment Committee.
  • Public Relation Officer – Harvard University African Health Forum.
  • Councillor – Harvard School of Public Health Alumni Association.
  • President - Harvard School of Public Health Class of 2011.
  • President – Harvard Nigerian Students and Scholars Society.
  • Chairman – Harvard School of Public Health 2011 World Malaria Day Organizing Committee.
  • Vice President – University of Ilorin Student Union Government.
  • President – Ilorin University Medical Student Association (ILUMSA).
  • Member – University of Ilorin Teaching Hospital SERVICOM Committee.
